## SOP 001 — Ticket Creation and Management

**Purpose:**
To ensure all customer interactions are accurately 
logged, categorised and managed in the CRM system.

**Scope:**
All customer support agents handling inbound 
and outbound interactions.

**Process Flow:**

    Customer Contacts Support
            ↓
    Agent Creates Ticket in CRM
            ↓
    Categorise and Set Priority
            ↓
    Assign to Correct Agent or Team
            ↓
    Work on Resolution
            ↓
    Issue Resolved?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Confirm     Escalate
    With        to Team
    Customer    Lead
       ↓          ↓
    Close       Follow
    Ticket      Up

**Procedure:**

Step 1 — Create Ticket
- Log into CRM system at start of shift
- Create a new ticket for every customer interaction
- Enter customer name, contact details and account ID
- Select correct ticket category and priority level
- Add brief description of the issue

Step 2 — Categorise and Prioritise
- Assign correct category — billing, technical, account etc
- Set priority level — low, medium, high, urgent
- Assign ticket to correct agent or team
- Set SLA deadline based on priority level

Step 3 — Update Ticket
- Update ticket status after every interaction
- Add detailed notes after every customer contact
- Attach relevant documents or screenshots
- Flag tickets approaching SLA deadline immediately

Step 4 — Close Ticket
- Confirm resolution with customer before closing
- Add final resolution notes to ticket
- Change status to Resolved then Closed
- Send CSAT survey to customer after closure

**Key Rules:**
- Never leave a ticket without a status update
- Never close a ticket without customer confirmation
- Always use correct category and priority level
- Document every interaction in real time

## SOP 002 — Escalation Handling

**Purpose:**
To ensure complex or sensitive customer issues are 
escalated correctly and resolved efficiently.

**Scope:**
All customer support agents and team leads.

**Escalation Triggers:**
- Issue cannot be resolved at agent level
- Customer is highly distressed or threatening
- Legal, compliance or fraud concerns raised
- SLA breach is imminent or has occurred
- Issue has been unresolved for more than 24 hours

**Process Flow:**

    Issue Identified
            ↓
    Within Agent Scope?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Resolve    Notify Customer
    Directly   of Escalation
       ↓          ↓
    Close      Escalate in CRM
    Ticket          ↓
               Brief Team Lead
                    ↓
               Team Lead Resolves
                    ↓
               Follow Up With Customer
                    ↓
               Close Ticket

**Procedure:**

Step 1 — Identify Escalation Need
- Assess if issue is within your scope to resolve
- Check if customer has contacted support before
- Review full CRM history before escalating
- Confirm escalation is necessary — not avoidable

Step 2 — Notify Customer
- Inform customer you are escalating their issue
- Provide realistic timeline for resolution
- Give customer a reference number
- Assure customer their issue is being prioritised

Step 3 — Escalate in CRM
- Change ticket priority to High or Urgent
- Add detailed escalation notes to ticket
- Tag Team Lead or relevant department
- Set escalation flag in CRM

Step 4 — Hand Over to Team Lead
- Brief Team Lead on full issue history
- Share all relevant documents and notes
- Confirm Team Lead has acknowledged escalation
- Remain available for follow up questions

Step 5 — Follow Up
- Check escalation status within 2 hours
- Update customer on progress proactively
- Confirm resolution with customer after escalation
- Document full escalation outcome in CRM

**Key Rules:**
- Never escalate without briefing the next person fully
- Always notify the customer before escalating
- Follow up on every escalation within 2 hours
- Document every escalation from start to finish

## SOP 003 — Customer Complaint Resolution

**Purpose:**
To ensure all customer complaints are handled 
professionally, empathetically and resolved efficiently.

**Scope:**
All customer support agents.

**Process Flow:**

    Customer Raises Complaint
            ↓
    Acknowledge and Apologise
            ↓
    Investigate in CRM
            ↓
    Root Cause Identified?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Resolve    Escalate
    Issue      to Specialist
       ↓          ↓
    Confirm    Follow Up
    With       Until
    Customer   Resolved
       ↓          ↓
    Close      Document
    Ticket     and Report

**Procedure:**

Step 1 — Acknowledge
- Allow customer to explain complaint fully
- Never interrupt or become defensive
- Acknowledge the complaint sincerely
- Apologise on behalf of the company genuinely

Step 2 — Investigate
- Review full CRM history immediately
- Identify root cause of the complaint
- Check if same issue has occurred before
- Gather all relevant information before responding

Step 3 — Resolve
- Provide clear and accurate solution
- Explain what happened and what is being done
- Give realistic resolution timeline
- Take ownership — do not transfer unnecessarily

Step 4 — Confirm
- Confirm customer is satisfied with resolution
- Ask if there is anything else you can help with
- Thank customer for bringing the issue to attention
- Send CSAT survey after resolution

Step 5 — Document and Report
- Log full complaint details in CRM
- Flag recurring complaints to Team Lead
- Relay feedback to relevant teams for improvement
- Follow up within 24 hours to confirm satisfaction

**Key Rules:**
- Never dismiss or minimise a customer complaint
- Always take ownership of the resolution
- Document every complaint fully in CRM
- Relay recurring issues to management immediately

## SOP 004 — SLA Breach Response

**Purpose:**
To ensure SLA breaches are identified, responded 
to and prevented from recurring.

**Scope:**
All customer support agents and team leads.

**Process Flow:**

    Review Ticket Queue Hourly
            ↓
    Ticket Approaching SLA Deadline?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Prioritise  Continue
    Immediately Normal Flow
       ↓
    SLA Already Breached?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Contact    Resolve
    Customer   Before
    Immediately Breach
       ↓          ↓
    Escalate   Update
    to Team    Ticket
    Lead            ↓
       ↓       Close
    Document   Ticket
    Breach
       ↓
    Prevent
    Recurrence

**Procedure:**

Step 1 — Identify Breach Risk
- Review ticket queue every hour
- Flag tickets approaching SLA deadline
- Prioritise at risk tickets immediately
- Notify Team Lead of potential breach

Step 2 — Respond to Breach
- Contact customer immediately to apologise
- Provide updated resolution timeline
- Escalate to Team Lead if not yet resolved
- Update ticket with breach notes and reason

Step 3 — Resolve Urgently
- Treat breached ticket as urgent priority
- Allocate additional resources if needed
- Resolve issue as quickly as possible
- Confirm resolution with customer personally

Step 4 — Document and Report
- Log breach details in CRM immediately
- Note reason for breach and actions taken
- Report breach to Team Lead same day
- Identify steps to prevent recurrence

Step 5 — Prevent Recurrence
- Review workload and ticket volume regularly
- Identify patterns in breach reasons
- Implement process improvements where needed
- Share learnings with team to prevent future breaches

**Key Rules:**
- Never ignore an approaching SLA deadline
- Always notify Team Lead of a breach immediately
- Contact customer proactively — never wait
- Document every breach with full details

## SOP 005 — End of Shift Handover

**Purpose:**
To ensure seamless transition between shifts with 
no tickets falling through the cracks.

**Scope:**
All customer support agents working shift patterns.

**Process Flow:**

    30 Minutes Before Shift End
            ↓
    Review All Open Tickets
            ↓
    Update Status on Every Ticket
            ↓
    Complete Handover Document
            ↓
    Brief Incoming Agent
            ↓
    Incoming Agent Confirms Receipt
            ↓
    Log Off All Systems Securely
            ↓
    Send Handover to Team Lead
            ↓
    Shift End Confirmed

**Procedure:**

Step 1 — Prepare Handover Notes
- Review all open tickets 30 minutes before shift end
- Update status on every open ticket
- Add notes on next steps for each unresolved ticket
- Flag urgent or sensitive cases clearly

Step 2 — Complete Handover Document

    Date: [DATE]
    Outgoing Agent: [NAME]
    Incoming Agent: [NAME]
    Shift: [START] to [END]

    Open Tickets: [NUMBER]
    Resolved This Shift: [NUMBER]
    Escalated This Shift: [NUMBER]

    Urgent Cases:
    - [Ticket ID] — [Issue] — [Next Step]

    Pending Follow Ups:
    - [Customer] — [Issue] — [Due By]

    System Issues:
    - [Any issues encountered]

    Notes for Incoming Agent:
    - [Important information]

Step 3 — Brief Incoming Agent
- Walk incoming agent through open tickets
- Highlight urgent and sensitive cases
- Confirm incoming agent has all information needed
- Answer any questions before signing off

Step 4 — Sign Off
- Confirm handover is complete in CRM
- Log off all systems securely
- Send handover document to Team Lead
- Confirm shift end time in system

**Key Rules:**
- Never leave without completing handover notes
- Always brief incoming agent personally where possible
- Flag urgent cases clearly — never assume they will be seen
- Send handover document to Team Lead every shift

## SOP 006 — Customer Verification

**Purpose:**
To ensure customer identity is verified before 
any sensitive account actions are taken.

**Scope:**
All customer support agents handling account requests.

**Process Flow:**

    Customer Makes Request
            ↓
    Standard Verification Required?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Verify     Process
    Name,      Request
    Email,     Immediately
    Phone
       ↓
    Enhanced Verification Required?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Request    Process
    Government Request
    ID              ↓
       ↓       Document
    Verification in CRM
    Successful?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Process    Escalate
    Request    to
       ↓       Compliance
    Document
    in CRM

**Standard Verification:**
- Confirm full name on account
- Confirm registered email address
- Confirm registered phone number
- Confirm last transaction amount or date

**Enhanced Verification — Required For:**
- Password reset requests
- Account number or email changes
- Large transaction disputes
- Account reactivation requests
- Any request flagged as high risk

**Enhanced Verification Steps:**
- Request government issued ID
- Confirm date of birth
- Confirm residential address
- Escalate to compliance if verification fails

**Key Rules:**
- Never skip verification for any account request
- Never process sensitive requests without full verification
- Escalate immediately if verification cannot be completed
- Document all verification attempts in CRM

## SOP 007 — Refund and Dispute Handling

**Purpose:**
To ensure all refund and dispute requests are 
handled fairly, efficiently and within policy.

**Scope:**
All customer support agents and disputes team.

**Process Flow:**

    Customer Raises Dispute
            ↓
    Verify Customer Identity
            ↓
    Collect Transaction Details
            ↓
    Investigate in System
            ↓
    Within Refund Policy?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Process    Escalate
    Refund     to Disputes
    Immediately Team
       ↓          ↓
    Give       Provide
    Reference  Timeline
    Number     3-5 Days
       ↓          ↓
    Follow     Follow
    Up         Up
       ↓          ↓
    Confirm    Confirm
    Receipt    Resolution
       ↓          ↓
    Close      Close
    Ticket     Ticket

**Procedure:**

Step 1 — Collect Information
- Confirm customer identity through verification
- Collect full transaction details
- Confirm amount, date and reference number
- Ask customer to explain dispute clearly

Step 2 — Investigate
- Verify transaction in system
- Check if transaction was processed correctly
- Review any previous interactions on the issue
- Confirm if refund is within policy guidelines

Step 3 — Resolve or Escalate
- If within policy — process refund immediately
- If outside policy — escalate to disputes team
- Provide customer with reference number
- Give realistic resolution timeline — 3 to 5 business days

Step 4 — Follow Up
- Confirm refund has been processed
- Contact customer to confirm receipt
- Document full outcome in CRM
- Close ticket after customer confirmation

**Key Rules:**
- Never process refund without full verification
- Always provide reference number to customer
- Escalate disputes outside your authority immediately
- Follow up on every refund until confirmed received

## SOP 008 — New Agent Onboarding

**Purpose:**
To ensure new customer support agents are onboarded 
efficiently and are ready to handle live interactions 
as quickly as possible.

**Scope:**
Team Leads and Senior Agents responsible for training.

**Process Flow:**

    New Agent Joins Team
            ↓
    Week 1 — Foundation Training
            ↓
    Week 2 — Observation and Shadowing
            ↓
    Week 3 — Supervised Live Interactions
            ↓
    Agent Ready for Independence?
       ↓          ↓
      YES          NO
       ↓          ↓
    Week 4 —   Extend
    Independent Supervised
    Handling   Training
       ↓          ↓
    Weekly     Daily
    KPI Review Feedback
       ↓
    Onboarding Complete
            ↓
    Document in CRM

**Onboarding Schedule:**

Week 1 — Foundation
- [ ] Introduce agent to team and tools
- [ ] Complete system access setup
- [ ] Walk through CRM and ticketing system
- [ ] Review company support policies and standards
- [ ] Share all SOPs and knowledge base documents
- [ ] Complete product knowledge training

Week 2 — Observation
- [ ] Agent shadows experienced agent for full week
- [ ] Agent observes live ticket handling
- [ ] Agent observes escalation handling
- [ ] Agent asks questions and takes notes
- [ ] Daily check in with Team Lead

Week 3 — Supervised Practice
- [ ] Agent handles live tickets with supervision
- [ ] Team Lead reviews every interaction
- [ ] Real time feedback provided after each ticket
- [ ] Agent attends team meetings and briefings
- [ ] Weekly performance review with Team Lead

Week 4 — Independent Handling
- [ ] Agent handles tickets independently
- [ ] Support available on request
- [ ] Weekly KPI review introduced
- [ ] Agent completes first self assessment
- [ ] Onboarding completion confirmed by Team Lead

**Key Rules:**
- Never rush the onboarding process
- Provide feedback daily during the first two weeks
- Celebrate early wins to build agent confidence
- Document onboarding completion in CRM
